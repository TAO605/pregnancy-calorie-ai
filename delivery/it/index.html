    Personalized Calorie Goals for Every Trimester

    This pregnancy calorie calculator uses age, height, pre-pregnancy weight, pregnancy week, pregnancy type, and daily activity level to estimate a daily calorie reference for pregnancy. It supports all trimesters and Weeks 1-42, so users can see how calorie needs may change as pregnancy progresses.

    Pregnancy nutrition matters because energy, protein, carbohydrates, fats, hydration, and steady meals all support maternal wellbeing and fetal growth. The result is designed as a practical conversation starter for users and their healthcare providers.

    To learn why the tool was built and how content is reviewed, read About Our AI Pregnancy Calorie Calculator. For support or content corrections, visit Contact Our Team.

    How Our AI Calculator Works

    The calculator estimates female BMR with the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, applies an activity factor to estimate TDEE, then adds a week-by-week pregnancy calorie curve for Weeks 1-42. It also shows IOM-style pregnancy weight gain reference ranges, a four-week calorie prediction, fetal development notes, and practical macro targets.

    After the calorie result is ready, the AI pregnancy nutrition tips use the same six inputs to create personalized diet, movement, and pregnancy reminder modules. Optional focus areas such as nausea, glucose-friendly meal planning, vegetarian meals, low appetite, or heartburn comfort can be added from the result page.

    Pregnancy calorie FAQ

    How many extra calories do I need during pregnancy?

    Many users need little to no extra energy early in pregnancy, then more in the second and third trimesters. This calculator shows a week-specific educational estimate based on your inputs.

    Does the calculator work for all trimesters?

    Yes. It supports Weeks 1-42 and shows trimester-aware calorie targets, macro ranges, and future four-week calorie predictions.

    Is this tool safe to use?

    It is safe as an educational planning tool, but it is not medical advice. Users with pregnancy complications or special care plans should review results with their healthcare provider.

    How accurate is this estimate?

    It is an educational estimate based on your inputs, IOM 2009 references, and a standard calorie formula. Your care team can personalize it further.

    Should I eat this exact number every day?

    No. Use it as a steady target range. Appetite, symptoms, activity, and medical advice can change what works best day to day.

    When should I ask my care team?

    Ask your doctor, midwife, or dietitian if your weight pattern changes quickly, you have diabetes or blood pressure concerns, or you have a special care plan.
