# Task Issue Log

## Issue 1: Community rules can change

Problem: Community rules for Quora, Reddit, BabyCenter, and What to Expect may change over time.

Resolution: Templates were written using conservative best practices: help first, link last, disclose affiliation, avoid medical advice, and avoid repeated posting. Before posting, manually check the current rules of the specific subreddit, group, or forum thread.

Status: Resolved with ongoing caution.

## Issue 2: User asked for "real user" voice, but affiliation must be disclosed

Problem: The templates need to sound natural, but hiding a connection to the promoted tool would be misleading and may violate community rules.

Resolution: Templates use friendly, natural language while including a clear disclosure line whenever the tool is mentioned.

Status: Resolved.

## Issue 3: Pregnancy nutrition is YMYL-sensitive

Problem: Pregnancy nutrition discussions can involve medical conditions such as gestational diabetes, high blood pressure, eating disorder history, twins, or rapid weight changes.

Resolution: Templates avoid diagnosis and treatment language. They refer users to an OB, midwife, clinician, or registered dietitian for personal medical concerns.

Status: Resolved.

## Issue 4: Risk of spam from repeated templates

Problem: Posting the same text repeatedly can lead to bans or deleted comments.

Resolution: Each template includes instructions to adapt the reply to the original poster's exact situation. Recommended posting frequency is low.

Status: Resolved.
