## Quora

### Template 1: Daily Calorie Needs During Pregnancy

Community: Quora  
Question type: How many calories do I need during pregnancy?  
Best use case: A broad informational question from someone asking about trimester calorie needs.

Reply:

Pregnancy calorie needs usually depend on your pre-pregnancy body size, activity level, and trimester. A common evidence-based starting point is that many people do not need much extra in the first trimester, then may need more energy in the second and third trimesters, but the exact number can vary a lot from person to person.

What helped me understand it better was looking at it as a baseline calorie estimate plus a pregnancy-stage adjustment, instead of thinking of it as simply "eating for two." It is also worth checking the number with your OB, midwife, or dietitian if you have nausea, gestational diabetes, twins, weight concerns, or appetite changes.

### Template 3: Pregnancy Weight Gain Standards

Community: Quora  
Question type: How much weight should I gain during pregnancy?  
Best use case: Questions about normal pregnancy weight gain range.

Reply:

Pregnancy weight gain guidance usually starts with pre-pregnancy BMI, because the recommended range is different for someone who was underweight, in the normal range, overweight, or obese before pregnancy. The goal is not to hit an exact number every week, but to stay within a healthy pattern over time.

If your gain feels faster or slower than expected, it is usually better to bring your weekly pattern to your doctor or midwife rather than judge yourself from one weigh-in. Fluid, constipation, nausea, appetite, and baby growth can all affect the number.
